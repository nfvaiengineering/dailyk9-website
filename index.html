How it works

One-on-one apprenticeship. Twelve to twenty-four months.

We start with the handler. We end with a working public-access team. Monthly milestones with named criteria — not vibes. Real timeline. Real cost. No surprises.

Phase 01

Foundation

Prospect selection or assessment of the dog you already have. Health and temperament vetting. Handler training begins before public-access training begins. We build the relationship and the basics.

Phase 02

Public-access progression

Graduated exposure to real environments — grocery stores, airports, medical settings. The handler learns to handle in public. The dog learns to work through it. Standards are explicit and measured.

Phase 03

Disability-specific tasks

The tasks the dog needs to perform for your specific disability are trained with you in the room. The dog learns to read you. You learn to read the dog. The team becomes one unit.

Phase 04

ASCT backed protection

The team graduates against ASCT-backed standards — credentials a stranger can verify and evaluations that mean something outside our walls. Daily K9 stays with the team for the life of the dog: refreshers, public-access tune-ups, and the standards-based backing that protects your working status wherever the work takes you.

Frequently asked

Before you book the call.

The questions most prospective handlers ask in the first ten minutes. Honest answers — the kind I'd want if I were the one calling.

Do I qualify? What counts as a disability?

The ADA defines a disability as a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities. A service dog is trained to perform specific tasks that mitigate that disability. We'll figure out together on the intake call whether your situation fits and whether a PSD or another type of service dog is the right path.

How much does the program cost?

A fraction of placement. Exact pricing depends on the dog, the disability, and the timeline — full numbers are discussed on the intake call so you can decide before any paperwork. There is always a path. We do not turn handlers away for ability to pay alone.

What if my dog doesn't pass the prospect assessment?

It happens, and it's better to know early. Not every dog is suited for service work — temperament, drive, and health all matter. If your current dog isn't a prospect, we tell you straight, and we help you find one that is.

How is this different from a $40,000 placement program?

You pick your dog. You build the relationship from day one. You are trained alongside the dog so you actually know how to handle. You don't wait five years. You don't pay forty thousand dollars. You do the work. The result is a tighter working team than placement typically produces.

Do I have to live in Maricopa County?

Local handlers get full in-person training. Out-of-state handlers can be supported with hybrid programs that combine intensive in-person blocks with remote coaching. We'll talk through whether the format works for your situation.

Will Daily K9 give my dog a certification?

The ADA does not require certification for service dogs. We do not provide registry paperwork. What we provide is a trained working team that meets real public-access standards. Anyone selling you a "registry" or "certification" is selling air.

What training methods do you use?

Balanced, real-world methods: positive reinforcement paired with clear, fair corrections — the right tool for the right dog, every time. No methodology religion, no gimmicks. Criteria-based training where the dog always understands the rules of the game.
